Khinkali â€” Georgian dough dumpling. It consists of a mixture of pork and beef (or lamb). It is a kind of bag made of dough, in which the minced meat is placed. It is prepared by boiling.

Recipe

In a large bowl, combine the flour, salt, eggs, and oil. Then add the water and knead to form a smooth dough. Place in a bowl, cover with a damp cloth, and let sit at room temperature for 1 hour. 2 Meanwhile, in a large bowl, combine the pork and beef, chopped onion, garlic, coriander, and pepper. Add the salt and pepper, and mix until well combined. 3 Once the dough is ready, roll it out to a thickness of 1-2 millimeters. Use a round cutter to make circles of dough. 4 Cut out circles with a round cutter. Stretch each circle slightly in diameter and place a filling, about 15 grams, in the center. Roll the filling into the dough so that it is completely covered. 5 Bring a large pot of water to a boil over high heat. Place each khinkali finger in the water and boil for 7-10 minutes. Once cooked, transfer to a bowl, sprinkle with pepper, and serve hot.

acharuli khachapuri

Adjarian khachapuri is a type of khachapuri. It is an oblong, oven-baked, hollowed-out mass filled with cheese. Typically, an egg is added to the top before serving.

Recipe

Mix the yeast, sugar and salt in the flour and pour in the milk, knead the soft dough and let it rise. Then roll it out into a round shape. Wrap it around both sides and tie the edges, give it a boat shape, brush the boats with egg yolk. Add one egg to the cheese and distribute this mixture in the boat shape. Put it in an oven preheated to 200 degrees. After 10 minutes, when it turns a little brown, remove it from the oven, make a hole in the center of the khachapuri with a knife and place a raw egg inside, return it to the oven for 5 minutes until the egg white sets. Remove the baked khachapuri from the oven, put a piece of butter on top and serve deliciously.
