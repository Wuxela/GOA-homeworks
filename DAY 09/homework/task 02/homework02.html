The Resident Evil




Resident Evil (Remake – 2002 / HD Remaster)

  • Original Release: 2002 (GameCube)
  • HD Remaster Release: 2015 (PS4, PS3, Xbox One, Xbox 360, PC, Nintendo Switch)
  • Genre: Survival Horror
  • Perspective: Third-person (fixed camera angles)
  • Developer/Publisher: Capcom

Playable Characters

You can choose between:

  • Chris Redfield
  • More health
  • Carries a lighter
  • Can’t carry as many items
  • No lockpick (must find keys manually)
  • Jill Valentine
  • Easier playthrough
  • Carries a lockpick
  • More inventory slots
  • Assisted by Barry Burton

Story Summary (No Major Spoilers)

  • You’re sent to investigate a missing team in the mountains.
  • You seek refuge in the mansion… but it’s crawling with undead creatures and biological experiments.
  • As you explore, you uncover the truth about the T-Virus, the Umbrella Corporation, and what really happened in the mansion.
  • The game focuses on survival, resource management, and puzzle solving, all while unraveling the conspiracy.

Key Features

  • Survival Horror Core:
  • Limited ammo and healing items
  • Inventory management (item boxes + limited slots)
  • Typewriters for saving (need ink ribbons!)
  • Fixed camera angles create tension and surprise

Crimson Heads (New to Remake)

  • If you don’t burn or destroy zombie bodies,
  • they return as faster,
  • tronger zombies later — adds fear and strategy.
  • Classic Puzzles:
  • Emblems, keys, statues, hidden doors, and secret passages.
  • Atmospheric Enemies:
  • Zombies, zombie dogs, Hunters, giant snakes, spiders, and more.

HD Remaster Improvements (2015)

  • Updated textures and lighting
  • Re-recorded voice acting
  • Modernized controls option (analog movement)
  • Released on modern consoles and PC

  • Despite being a remaster of a 2002 game,
  • it still looks and plays great,
  • and it’s one of the best horror experiences ever made

Why It’s So Loved

  • Considered the perfect remake — expands the original 1996 game while respecting it.
  • Incredible atmosphere, level design, and suspense.
  • Holds up as a masterclass in survival horror even today.
  • Inspired future horror games like Dead Space, The Evil Within, and even Resident Evil 7.
