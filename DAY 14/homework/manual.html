What is a Manual Gearbox?
A manual gearbox (also known as a manual transmission) is a system in a car that allows the driver to manually change gears using a gear stick and a clutch pedal.

How Does It Work?
The driver presses the clutch pedal to disconnect the engine from the wheels.
Then uses the gear stick to select the desired gear (e.g., 1st, 2nd, 3rd, 4th, 5th, Reverse).
After shifting, the driver releases the clutch, and the power goes back to the wheels.

Main Parts:
Clutch pedal – lets you temporarily disconnect the engine from the gearbox.
Gear stick (or shifter) – used to choose the gear.
Gearbox – contains the gear sets that control power and speed.

Advantages of Manual Gearbox:
More control over the car.
Often better fuel efficiency (in some cases).
Cheaper to maintain and repair.
Preferred in racing and performance driving.
